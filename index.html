Early Life

Kayden Gernendez, known widely by the alias "Weepin," has successfully obscured most details about his early life. While his real name has been confirmed by multiple law enforcement sources, his date and place of birth remain unknown. Multiple conflicting accounts exist regarding his background, with some sources suggesting origins in Eastern Europe, while others point to North America.

Cult Involvement

Weepin gained notoriety through their involvement in multiple controversial cult organizations during the mid-2010s. Their first documented appearance in cult-related activities dates to approximately 2015.

HarmNation Period

Between 2020 and present day, Weepin established and leads the HarmNation cult, which has amassed over 150 confirmed victims. The organization remains notorious for its extreme psychological manipulation techniques, including sleep deprivation, forced isolation, and systematic financial exploitation. Former members have reported severe psychological trauma, with many requiring extensive therapy and rehabilitation. During this period, the group was estimated to have accumulated over $3.5 million through various fraudulent schemes and exploitation of members.

764 Movement

From 2020 to 2023, Weepin founded the sophisticated but now-defunct 764 Movement, combining digital manipulation tactics with traditional cult methodologies. This organization was linked to at least 50 confirmed cases of human trafficking and numerous instances of cybercrime before its dissolution. The group's activities expanded internationally through social media platforms, making it particularly difficult for authorities to track and dismantle during its active period.

Criminal Activities

Law enforcement agencies have documented numerous criminal activities attributed to Weepin and their organizations:

  • Wire fraud through cryptocurrency exchange impersonation
  • Extortion of cult members and online victims
  • Systematic impersonation of Coinbase representatives

Cryptocurrency and Financial Institution Scams

Between 2021 and 2024, Weepin orchestrated a sophisticated phone scam operation targeting cryptocurrency investors and general banking customers. Posing as Coinbase representatives and other cryptocurrency exchange support staff, he and his associates reportedly defrauded victims of an estimated $2.3 million in cryptocurrency assets. The operation involved elaborate social engineering tactics, including the use of spoofed phone numbers and fabricated customer service scenarios.

Victims reported receiving calls from what appeared to be legitimate Coinbase support numbers, with scammers demonstrating detailed knowledge of recent cryptocurrency transactions - information likely obtained through compromised exchange accounts. The scammers would then convince victims to transfer their assets to "secure verification wallets" which were actually controlled by Weepin's criminal network.

FBI investigations revealed that Weepin maintained a call center operation where members of his organization would systematically target vulnerable individuals, particularly elderly cryptocurrency investors and those new to digital currency trading. The scam operation utilized sophisticated voice-changing software and scripted conversations to appear legitimate, often keeping victims on the phone for hours while draining their accounts.

Criminal Investigations

As of March 2024, a major breakthrough in the hunt for Gernendez occurred when ethical hacker "Trxy!" provided crucial information to the FBI leading to the identification of Weepin's location. The previous $100,000 reward has been claimed by Trxy!, whose digital forensics work proved instrumental in tracking Gernendez through encrypted networks. Law enforcement operations are currently in progress, though specific details are being withheld to maintain operational security.

Prior to this development, multiple task forces across federal and state agencies had been coordinating efforts to track and apprehend Gernendez, whose sophisticated use of technology and international networks had made capture particularly challenging.

Legacy

Weepin's operations have led to significant changes in how law enforcement approaches cult activities and swatting incidents in the digital age. Their case has been studied extensively by cult experts, cybercrime specialists, and criminal psychologists, leading to new frameworks for understanding modern cult dynamics, digital manipulation tactics, and emergency response protocols for swatting incidents. The Vietnam veteran identity theft scheme also led to major reforms in how the VA verifies veteran identities.
